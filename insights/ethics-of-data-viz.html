The Ethics of Data Visualisation

Category: Ethics · Published: Oct 2025

One of the most critical challenges in information design is balancing accuracy, impact, and our responsibility as designers. A visualisation is not a perfect mirror of reality; it is a portrayal, shaped by the designer's choices, biases, and goals.

Intent and Interpretation

Every chart, graph, and map tells a story. An ethical designer's first duty is to ensure that story is a truthful representation of the underlying data. It is easy to mislead, whether intentionally or not. For example, changing a y-axis scale can exaggerate a trend, while choosing a specific colour can provoke an emotional response that the data itself does not support.

Clarity vs. Persuasion

There is a fine line between making data clear and making it persuasive. Our goal should always be clarity. An effective visualisation gives the audience the power to draw their own conclusions. A manipulative one forces a specific conclusion upon them. The final design must serve the truth of the data, not the agenda of the presenter. This requires a commitment to transparency, context, and intellectual honesty.
