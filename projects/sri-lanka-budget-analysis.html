Sri Lanka Government Budget Analysis

A fiscal data visualisation initiative designed to improve public understanding and government transparency by transforming dense government publications into accessible visual narratives.

The Challenge

National budgets are typically published as dense, inaccessible documents. This makes it difficult for the public and even policymakers to understand key fiscal priorities, spending allocations, and revenue sources. The core challenge was to simplify this complexity into a clear and engaging format without losing important details or sacrificing accuracy.

How can we empower citizens with the knowledge to participate in fiscal discourse if the information itself is locked away in jargon and complex tables?

My Role & Process

As the lead Information Designer, I was responsible for the entire visual strategy. My work involved analysing the raw budget data, designing a cohesive visual language, and developing a series of straightforward infographics and charts. I acted as the bridge between the raw data and public understanding. The process included data cleaning, narrative structuring, and iterative design to ensure clarity and impact.

One of the key outputs was an animated video that broke down the budget components, designed for maximum shareability on social media platforms.

The Outcome

The resulting visualisations were widely shared on social media and used in policy briefs by researchers and journalists. The project made complex fiscal information accessible, fostering a more informed public debate on national spending priorities and demonstrating how strategic design can make crucial government data available to everyone.
